## Claim-scope rules
Use cautious descriptive language:
- index behaviour
- distributional profiles
- descriptive sensitivity
- corpus-specific patterns
- cross-corpus patterns

Avoid:
- direct L1/L2 population claims
- strong developmental progression claims
- causal claims
- claims of greater cohesion sophistication
- Random Forest or predictive modelling in the main manuscript
- Kruskal-Wallis, Spearman, or Dunn posthoc results in the main Results section

## Corpus-design caution
Any cross-corpus interpretation must acknowledge differences in:
- task
- genre
- mode
- sampling
- proficiency or educational level
- writer population
- L1 background
- text length
- paragraph reliability

## Reporting requirements
Where results are discussed, include dispersion when possible:
- mean
- median
- SD
- IQR
- minimum
- maximum
- zero-density cases when relevant
